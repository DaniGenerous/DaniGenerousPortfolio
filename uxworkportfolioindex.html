Ux Design Work

Dukes Demand Deals

SMAD 203 UX Research Project — I interviewed students around JMU’s campus to gather data on how many students regularly visit our historical downtown area. With that data, I created an app prototype that would encourage students to go downtown by advertising the various student-discounts offered by the businesses in that area.

Reevaluation of JMU’s Campus Dish

SMAD 203 User Research Project — For this project, I imagined I had been hired to perform a usability study for JMU’s dining website,“Campus Dish“. I proposed concepts for improving the overall user experience of the website through the creation of static wireframes and mockups of additional features that I think would vastly improve users’ experience on the site.

Personas are fictional characters that represent a specific segment of a user base, developed based on research and data about real users. Personas help to bring user needs and behaviors to life, providing a clear understanding of who the users are, what their goals are, and what motivates them. Above you'll find three personas that I have created for various projects for my UX design courses at JMU.


Storyboarding

Storyboarding is a technique used in UX design to visually communicate a sequence of events or interactions that a user might experience while using a product or service. It is an effective way to convey design concepts, test ideas, and identify potential issues early on in the design process. By creating a series of sketches or illustrations, storyboarding helps to bring design ideas to life, and enables designers to better understand how users might interact with a product or service. These are two storyboard's I created while in the research stage of our app development for SMAD 317.
